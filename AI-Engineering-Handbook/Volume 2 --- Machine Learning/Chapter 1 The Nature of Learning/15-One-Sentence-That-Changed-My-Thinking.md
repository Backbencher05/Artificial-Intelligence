# What These Sentences Really Mean

When I read these sentences one by one, I notice something surprising.

The chapter wasn't really about Machine Learning.

It was about building an engineering mindset.

Without realizing it, I learned to think differently.

I stopped asking:

* "Which algorithm should I use?"

and started asking:

* "What problem am I trying to solve?"

I stopped believing that:

* More data automatically means more learning.

and started understanding that:

* Patterns create intelligence—not stored examples.

I stopped seeing feedback as criticism.

I started seeing feedback as guidance.

I stopped chasing perfection.

I started chasing meaningful improvement.

These changes are more valuable than memorizing definitions because they influence every engineering decision I will make in the future.

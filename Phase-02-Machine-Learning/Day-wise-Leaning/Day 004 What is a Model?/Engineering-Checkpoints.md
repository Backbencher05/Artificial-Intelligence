# Engineering Checkpoint

## 1. What new mental model did I build today?

Today, I understood that a model is not an exact copy of reality. It is a simplified representation created for a specific purpose, keeping only the information needed to solve a particular problem.

---

## 2. What misconception did I correct today?

Earlier, I thought a good model should include as much detail as possible. Today, I realized that adding unnecessary details can actually make a model less useful. A good model is simple enough to be practical while still capturing what matters.

---

## 3. What question is still confusing me?

I understand that a model simplifies reality, but I'm curious about how we decide which details are important to keep and which ones can safely be ignored.

---

## 4. If I had to teach today's lesson in 2 minutes, what would I say?

Imagine you're visiting a new city.

Would you carry the entire city on your back?

Of course not.

Instead, you carry a map.

The map doesn't show every tree, every parked car, or every person walking on the street. It keeps only the information that helps you reach your destination.

That's why it's useful.

Models work in the same way.

Whenever we want to understand or solve a complex problem, we first create a simplified version that focuses on what matters and ignores what doesn't. The goal isn't to copy reality perfectly—it's to make reality easier to understand and work with.

That's what a model really is: **a simplified representation of reality created for a specific purpose.**

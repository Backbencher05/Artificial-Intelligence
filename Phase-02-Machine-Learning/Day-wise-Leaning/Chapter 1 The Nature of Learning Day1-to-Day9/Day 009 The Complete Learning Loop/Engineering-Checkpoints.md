## 4. If I had to teach today's lesson in 2 minutes, what would I say?

Imagine you're learning to ride a bicycle.

Your first ride isn't perfect.

You wobble, lose balance, and maybe even fall.

But after each attempt, you notice what happened. You make a small adjustment, try again, and gradually improve.

You don't become an expert in one ride.

You improve because you repeat the same cycle:

**Try → Observe → Improve → Try Again.**

This same pattern appears everywhere—in cooking, sports, coding, public speaking, and learning a language.

Machine Learning follows this exact idea. Instead of expecting perfect results immediately, it improves step by step through repeated attempts and continuous feedback.

The important lesson isn't that machines learn differently from us.

It's that **learning itself is a process**, whether it's happening in a person or in a machine.

# Part A — Models Around You

## 1. Google Maps

### What is the real-world system?

An entire city with roads, buildings, parks, traffic, and people.

### What is the model?

A digital map of the city.

### What important details does the model keep?

Roads, directions, distances, landmarks, and locations.

### What details does it ignore?

Building interiors, trees, people walking, weather, and many small objects.

### Why is the simplified model still useful?

Because it provides exactly the information needed to navigate without overwhelming the user with unnecessary details.

# Part B — Think Like an Engineer

## Q1. Why can't a model contain every detail about reality?

Reality is incredibly complex, with countless details changing all the time. Including every detail would make the model just as complicated as reality itself, making it difficult to understand or use. A model is useful because it focuses only on what is important for its purpose.

---

## Q2. Why is simplification actually a strength rather than a weakness?

Simplification removes unnecessary details and highlights the information that matters most. This makes the model easier to understand, faster to use, and more practical for solving a specific problem.

---

## Q3. What's the difference between reality and a model of reality?

Reality contains everything that exists, whether it's relevant or not. A model is a simplified version that captures only the essential parts needed for a particular task. It is not reality itself—it is a useful representation of reality.

---

## Q4. Based on everything we've learned so far, how would you explain the word "model" to someone with no ML background?

Imagine you're planning a road trip.

You don't carry the entire city with you—you carry a map.

The map isn't the city.

It doesn't show every tree, every person, or every building.

It only shows the information you need to reach your destination.

A model works the same way.

It isn't the real thing. It's a simplified representation that keeps the important information and leaves out unnecessary details so it can help us make decisions or solve problems.

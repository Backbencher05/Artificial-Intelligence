# Engineering Checkpoint

## 1. What new mental model did I build today?

Today, I learned that effective learning is a balancing act. Improving too slowly can delay progress, while changing too much can create new problems. The best results often come from steady, well-controlled improvements.

---

## 2. What misconception did I correct today?

Earlier, I believed that making bigger changes would always lead to faster improvement. Today, I realized that both extremes—too much change and too little change—can slow down learning. The goal is to find the right balance.

---

## 3. What question is still confusing me?

I understand that learning should involve balanced adjustments, but I'm curious about how a machine decides whether an adjustment was too large, too small, or just right.

---

## 4. If I had to teach today's lesson in 2 minutes, what would I say?

Imagine you're trying to adjust the water temperature for a shower.

If the water is slightly cold and you turn the hot water all the way up, it quickly becomes too hot.

If you barely turn the knob at all, the water stays cold and you don't solve the problem.

The best approach is to make a small, controlled adjustment, check the temperature, and adjust again if needed.

Many everyday activities work this way. Whether you're learning to drive, improving your fitness, studying for an exam, or fixing a recipe, success usually comes from making balanced changes, observing the results, and continuing to improve step by step.

Learning isn't about making the biggest change possible. It's about making the **right** change at the **right** time and repeating that process until you reach your goal.

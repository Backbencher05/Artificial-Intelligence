# Engineering Perspective

Today we learned that **repeated observation is essential for learning**.

Whether it's a person, a software system, or a real-world process, a single example only provides a snapshot. It may capture an unusual case or miss important variations. By observing many examples over time, we begin to identify what is consistent and what changes.

This way of thinking is valuable in every area of engineering. Good decisions are rarely based on one observation—they are based on recognizing patterns across many observations.

> **Reliable understanding comes from patterns, not isolated examples.**

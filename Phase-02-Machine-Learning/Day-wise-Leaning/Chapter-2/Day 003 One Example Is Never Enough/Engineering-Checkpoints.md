# Engineering Checkpoint

## 1. What new mental model did I build today?

I learned that understanding a concept requires observing many examples rather than relying on a single instance. Repeated exposure helps reveal patterns that one example cannot show.

---

## 2. What misconception did I correct?

I used to think one example could be enough to understand something. Now I realize that one example may represent only a small part of the overall picture and can even be misleading.

---

## 3. What question is still confusing me?

How many examples are usually enough before we can confidently understand or represent a concept?

---

## 4. If I had to teach today's lesson in 2 minutes, what would I say?

A single example rarely captures the full nature of a concept because real-world objects and situations have many variations. By observing many examples, we begin to recognize what stays consistent despite those differences. Learning is the process of discovering these consistent patterns through repeated experience.

---

## 🔍 One Sentence That Changed My Thinking

> **"One example tells a story; many examples reveal a pattern."**

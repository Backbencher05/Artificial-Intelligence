
# Engineering Checkpoint

## 1. What new mental model did I build today?

I learned that collecting information alone is not enough. To make information useful, it must be organized in a structured way so that it can be searched, compared, and analyzed efficiently.

---

## 2. What misconception did I correct?

I used to think that having lots of data was enough. Now I understand that without proper organization, even valuable data becomes difficult to use and can lead to poor decisions.

---

## 3. What question is still confusing me?

What are the best ways to organize very large collections of information so they remain efficient as they continue to grow?

---

## 4. If I had to teach today's lesson in 2 minutes, what would I say?

Real-world information often consists of many individual examples. These examples become useful only when they are organized in a consistent structure. Good organization allows us to search, compare, summarize, and discover patterns efficiently, making better decisions possible.

# Part A — Everyday Balance

## 1. Dieting

### What is the goal?

To lose weight in a healthy and sustainable way.

### What happens if the adjustment is too small?

Progress is very slow, and it may feel like nothing is changing.

### What happens if the adjustment is too large?

Eating too little can lead to low energy, poor nutrition, and difficulty maintaining the diet.

### What would a balanced adjustment look like?

Making gradual changes to food habits while maintaining proper nutrition.

### How is this similar to Machine Learning?

Improvement happens through steady, controlled adjustments rather than extreme changes.

# Part B — Think Like an Engineer

## Q1. Why isn't "change as much as possible" always the best strategy?

Large changes can create new problems or move us farther away from the goal. They also make it difficult to understand whether the change actually improved the situation.

---

## Q2. Why isn't "change as little as possible" always the best strategy?

If the changes are too small, progress becomes very slow. In some situations, meaningful improvement requires adjustments that are noticeable enough to move closer to the goal.

---

## Q3. Why do you think learning often happens through many small improvements instead of one perfect change?

Most real-world problems are too complex to solve perfectly in one attempt. Small improvements allow us to learn from each step, observe the results, and continue improving without creating unnecessary risks.

---

## Q4. Based on today's lesson, what qualities would you expect from a good learning process?

A good learning process should:

* Improve gradually instead of making extreme changes.
* Learn from feedback after every attempt.
* Make thoughtful adjustments rather than random ones.
* Be consistent and stable over time.
* Move closer to the goal with each improvement.

Q1. Why is consistency more important than intensity?

Answer:

Consistency is more important than intensity because AI mastery is built through continuous learning and practice over a long period of time, not through short bursts of extreme effort.

Reasons:

AI is a vast field that includes Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Generative AI, LLMs, RAG systems, agents, deployment, and system design.
Trying to learn everything quickly often leads to:
Burnout
Information overload
Lack of retention
Inconsistent progress
Small daily efforts compound over time.

For example:

Studying 2 hours every day for a year is far more effective than studying 12 hours a day for a few weeks and then stopping.

Consistency helps build:

Strong fundamentals
Long-term discipline
Real project experience
Sustainable growth

Conclusion:

Consistency creates lasting expertise, while intensity without sustainability often leads to burnout and abandoned goals.

Q2. Why should we focus on fundamentals instead of frameworks?

Answer:

We should focus on fundamentals because frameworks change rapidly, but core concepts remain valuable for decades.

Examples of fundamentals:

Examples of frameworks:

Frameworks evolve constantly:

Frameworks come and go.

If I understand the fundamentals:

I avoid becoming dependent on specific tools.

Conclusion:

Fundamentals teach me why things work, while frameworks only teach me how to use current tools.
